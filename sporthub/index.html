About Sports Authority of Goa

The Sports Authority of Goa (SAG) is an autonomous body, registered under the Societies Registration Act 1860, and is fully financed by the Government of Goa. SAG is dedicated to rendering commendable services for the development of sports and games across the state.

SAG plays a crucial role in enhancing sports infrastructure, including the construction and upgrading of various venues for major events such as the 37th National Games held in Goa in 2023. This commitment extends to ensuring facilities meet the highest standards of respective federation guidelines.

Currently, SAG is focused on drafting a new State Sports Policy 2025. This initiative aims to update the existing Sports Policy 2009, with a strong emphasis on improving coaching standards, maintaining infrastructure, planning for the legacy of the National Games, and efficient sports equipment management.

Goa is actively positioning itself as a premier sports tourism destination, attracting and hosting significant international events like the Chess World Cup and the Asian Gymnastics Championship. These efforts underscore SAG's dedication to fostering a vibrant sports culture and promoting Goa on the global sports map.
